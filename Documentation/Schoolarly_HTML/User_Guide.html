1. Welcome page

Once you have accesed the ODa application, you will be welcomed with the main page of the application.

2. Create an user account (Register into application)

In ODa you are able to create an user account (to register) in order to perform several actions across the application. You can do it by clicking 2 button from the web page:

In order to register in the ODa application, you have to complete the registration form by inserting your full name, your email address and a password. After these, you must agree with the fact that the website could use your data if needed.

3. Login

Once you have successfully registered in the ODa application, you will be able to log in it with your credentials.

4. Visualisation

Once you have successfully logged in the ODa application, you will be able to perform 2 actions:

  • Visualize data from a SPARQL query on a public dataset specified by you;
  • View all your saved queries, including those ones received from your friends;

4.1 Visualize data access options

You can query an open dataset with a SPARQL query thru ODa application. To do this, you have to click on one of the 2 buttons from the page:

4.2 Execute query

In the visualize page of the ODa application, you can choose between:

  • Specifying the public dataset in the top text box and writing the query in the main text box of the page (option 1 in image)
  • Choosing one of our default saved queris from the top right corner select in the page (option 2 in image)

Once you finished writing your SPARQL query, you can execute it by clicking on the button "Run" from the top right corner of the page.

4.3 Choose chart

Once you have written your SPARQL query or chosen one of our default queries, you have to choose which type of chart you want to use in order to be represented the data retrieved from the dataset.
Info: At the top of the page will be written an AI generated recommandation to visualize and a description of your data.

4.4 View plain data

You can choose to see the retrieved data in a tabular view by selecting the file "Table" top of the page

4.5 Visualize data and export json

After you chose the type of chart you want to visualize, you have to select which fields need to be included in the graphic representation from the 2 select boxes as shown in the picture "View chart". As well as visualizing, you can save your visualization in json type by clicking the button "Export JSON" from the top of the page.

4.6 Save visualisation

Once you have the view of the selected visualization with its fields, you can save this graphical representation in your account and access it later if you need.

4.7 View saved visualizations

You can access "My visualizations" section of the application by selecting it from the right side menu of the webpage. In this section are included your own saved graphical representations and received representations from your friends.

4.8 Share visualization

In the view page of a visualization, you can choose to share that visualisation by clicking the button "Share" from the top right corner of the page and writing friend's email in the pop-up box.
